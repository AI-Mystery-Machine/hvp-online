Hey! I'm Hiren

I am a Statistician turned Data Scientist based in London, UK and I am passionate about all things Data, Statistics/Machine Learning and Cloud Computing. Thank you for taking the time to get to know me . . .

My Journey

I started my career as a Statistician supporting the development of FMCG and Drug products, before going on to enter the world of Data/ML Consulting where I help organisations embrace Data, Machine Learning, and Cloud Technology. To truly understand my journey and how I got to where I am today, we need to go all the way back to the start . . .


Sept 2006

I enter Brunel University to study Financial Mathematics. Why Financial Mathematics? Well, at this point, all I knew was that if you were good at Maths, you'd go on to work in Finance. After completing my first two years of studies, I realised that a career in Finance was not for me. Therefore, for my third year, I secured a 1-year internship with the Statistics department at Unilever.

Aug 2008

During my internship with Unilever, I was mentored by talented Senior Statisticians, I learned how to program in SAS and I statistically supported numerous R&D studies. My time with Unilever inspired me to pursue a career as a Statistician and I was advised by my mentors to complete an MSc in Statistics.

Sept 2009

I returned to Brunel University to complete my final year of undergraduate studies. Naturally, my final year modules and dissertation topic were all focused on Applied Statistics. I also applied and secured my place to study for an MSc in Statistics at Warwick University starting in September 2011.

Nov 2010

After graduating with a BSc in Financial Mathematics from Brunel University, I took on a short-term role with the Royal Bank of Scotland (RBS). I provided analytics support for their recently launched Natwest Mobile Banking app before I headed to Warwick University to begin my Master's studies.

Sept 2011

During my studies at Warwick University, I especially enjoyed my modules in Medical Statistics, Biostatistics, and Bayesian Statistics which piqued my interest in a career in the Pharmaceutical industry. I completed my final year dissertation (on the Analysis of Gene Expression Data) and graduated in August 2012.

May 2013

I finally achieved my goal of becoming a Statistician when I joined GlaxoSmithKline (GSK). I supported the product development of respiratory drugs and go on to lead a small team of Jnr. Statisticians. After two years, my curiosity grew about the applications of Statistics outside of FMCG/Drug R&D.

May 2015 - Present

My curiosity led me to join Accenture's Data Science Centre of Excellence (CoE). With a new focus on Data Science consulting, I would help Accenture's clients embrace Data, Machine Learning, and Cloud Technology. Over the next 6 years, my role and responsibilities grew, which brings us to today . . .

Today, I lead large multidisciplinary technical teams to help organisations create intelligent value-adding data products/services. I have guided organisations from various industries to create their data science roadmap, define their ML/Cloud strategy, architect their cloud platform and create/deploy their first machine learning solution. With a focus on the complete data product life-cycle, I specialise in Analytics Strategy, Machine Learning, Cloud Technology, and DevOps.
